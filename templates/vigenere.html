Vigenère description

Type: Polyalphabetic substitution cipher (many “Caesar” shifts in one message).

Strength: Much stronger than a single Caesar shift, but still breakable. With a short or repeated key, cryptanalysts use letter frequencies, the index of coincidence, and repeated patterns (Kasiski) to guess the key length and then each shift.

How it works: Choose a keyword. A–Z are treated as numbers 0–25. Each plaintext letter is shifted by the value of the next keyword letter; the keyword wraps when it runs out. Encrypt: add the key letter (mod 26); decrypt: subtract it (mod 26). Spaces and punctuation are usually left unchanged (this app follows that rule and only advances the key on letters).

Key: A word or phrase; longer, less predictable keys are harder to crack. There is no separate public/private key—anyone who knows the keyword can encrypt or decrypt.

Use: Historical cipher and teaching example; not used for serious security today.
